The perks of mortality

March 20, 2024 ⋅ 142 words

Ross Gay, Scott Galloway, and Steve Jobs all use mortality as a tool for better living.

Ross Gay says that remembering the inevitibility of death helps us love better, find joy together, and forgive ourselves a little more.

Scott Galloway says that our mortality creates an inherent need in humans to be part of something bigger. It gives us permission to not give a shit what people think because we're going to die and no one is going to remember anything. It enables us to be more generous and charitable.

Steve Jobs says that death is the greatest invention of life because it gives us the courage to take risks and follow our intuition. It helps us prioritize because "almost everything — all external expectations, all pride, all fear of embarrassment or failure - these things just fall away in the face of death."
